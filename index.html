Welcome to my presentation

Hi! I'm Morgan Pigeron, on this page I will give you some info to be able to understand who I am without taking too much of your time ⏰!

Privilege to learn with Thales Alenia Space

Thales gave me the chance to be in apprenticeship in 2012 as a mechanical part designer. Designing tool used for the assembly or test of satellite subsystem was my job. That was the opportunity to learn a lot from space context and all the domain involved.

Passionate about new technologies

In 2013, I've discover the world of mechatronic by participating in an online challenge with two of my classmate. The project was to develop an entire 3d printer from scratch. We finished 5th of 60+ others teams.

Motivation and career evolution

After three years as a mechanical part designer, The time as came to take a big decision, continue my studies. Recruited by the R&D office : futur products, mechanisms and solar arrays, I was in apprenticeship as mechatronic engineer. This tree years were very interesting, I learned a lot about innovation and I was finally able to express myself in the three domains: mechanic, electronic and computing. In different use case : Prototyping, designing, controlling, testing etc..

Stand on your own feet

Six years in Thales Alenia Space gave me the motivation to become a freelancer. This was a really great experience to work with local companies, providing them solution in mechanic and embedded electronic. This two years experience is for me a big step forward for my autonomy and project management skills. PCB designing, microcontroller programming and mechanical part designing was the heart of this experience.

What's next ?

It's time for me to learn again. I'm looking for a company that will give me the chance to improve and learn new skills. Since I'm passionate about new technology I'm available to any propositions that include working on project that involve latest technology!

Lidar driver for nodeJs

NodeJs can be run on single board computer with Arm Cpu, so I've tested it on Raspberry Pi zero. Cannot process data from the lidar as fast as in C++, but it's working pretty well. Data visualization is made with P5.js connected through websocket.
